Do I Need a PTSD Service Dog?

Written by: Dr. Meg Colley

https://www.pawsitivityservicedogs.com/ptsd

“82% of those with a PTSD diagnosis reported symptom reduction after a partnership with a service dog.”

There are not many great treatments yet for PTSD. And coping with those treatments is a task in itself. One option is to get a PTSD service dog. These dogs are trained to help with over-active startle responses by alerting you to presence of others (i.e., ‘pop a corner’ or ‘watch my back’). PTSD service dogs also can help with hyper-vigilance, as the dog provides an environmental threat assessment. They can even help with flashbacks, dissociation, and hallucinations, as they can improve reality testing, keeping you grounded and in the real world, not lost in your mind.

“A PTSD service dog is especially helpful when combined with cognitive-behavioral therapy from a therapist or psychologist, and it is a therapeutic technique that is repeatedly demonstrated to be legitimate and effective. Applying cognitive-behavioral skills to interactions with a service dog can produce powerful outcomes.”

Personally, I think having a service dog might help me with my own PTSD. And I’m not talking “emotional support animal” here. I want a fully trained service animal. I want one that’s trained to find me and help bring me back to reality when I’m having a flashback or dissociating. One who will lie next to me and provide active sensory stimuli to keep my mind in the present. One who is trained to alert me to true danger, so my mind can rest and turn off the constant hypervigilance.

One who can come out with me in public, to help keep me grounded when I dissociate or start to experience flashbacks. Maybe I would be less scared to be out in public if I had help to keep me grounded, to help my mind realize that the people around me are not dangerous. So I dont flip out on someone or have to run away in a sudden panic.

Not gonna lie, having a PTSD service dog might be a good thing.
